Sustainable Development Goals
India


Overview

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), also known as the Global Goals, were adopted by the United Nations in 2015 as a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that by 2030 all people enjoy peace and prosperity.

The 17 SDGs are integrated—they recognize that action in one area will affect outcomes in others, and that development must balance social, economic and environmental sustainability. Countries have committed to prioritize progress for those who're furthest behind. The SDGs are designed to end poverty, hunger, AIDS, and discrimination against women and girls.


The creativity, knowhow, technology and financial resources from all of society is necessary to achieve the SDGs in every context.


The 17 SDGs are:

  1. No Poverty: End poverty in all its forms everywhere.

  2. Zero Hunger: End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture.

  3. Good Health and Well-being: Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages.

  4. Quality Education: Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all.
  5. Gender Equality: Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.

  6. Clean Water and Sanitation: Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.

  7. Affordable and Clean Energy: Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all.

  8. Decent Work and Economic Growth: Promote sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment and decent work for all.

  9. Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure: Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization and foster innovation.

  10. Reduced Inequalities: Reduce inequality within and among countries.

  11. Sustainable Cities and Communities: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable.

  12. Responsible Consumption and Production: Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns.

  13. Climate Action: Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.

  14. Life Below Water: Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development.

  15. Life On Land: Protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and halt biodiversity loss.

  16. Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions: Promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all and build effective, accountable and inclusive institutions at all levels.

  17. Partnerships for the Goals: Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development.

Each of these goals includes specific targets and indicators to measure progress, and they are all interconnected, meaning progress in one goal can have positive effects on others. Achieving the SDGs requires collective action from governments, civil society, the private sector, and individuals, and a commitment to leave no one behind.



Sustainable Development Goals: India

India, a member of the UN, is critical in determining the success of the SDGs, globally. At the UN Sustainable Development Summit in 2015, Prime Minister Narendra Modi noted, “Sustainable development of one-sixth of humanity will be of great consequence to the world and our beautiful planet. It will be a world of fewer challenges and greater hope; and, more confident of its success”. NITI Aayog, the Government of India’s premier think tank, has been entrusted with the task of coordinating the SDGs, mapping schemes related to the SDGs and their targets, and identifying lead and supporting ministries for each target. In addition, the Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI) has been leading discussions for developing national indicators for the SDGs. State governments are key to India’s progress on the SDGs as they are best placed to ‘put people first’ and to ensuring that ‘no one is left behind’. The UN Country Team in India supports NITI Aayog, Union ministries and state governments in their efforts to address the interconnectedness of the goals, to ensure that no one is left behind and to advocate for adequate financing to achieve the SDGs.


What is NITI Ayog ?

The NITI Aayog (National Institution for Transforming India) serves as the apex public policy think tank of the Government of India, and the nodal agency tasked with catalyzing economic development, and fostering cooperative federalism through the involvement of State Governments of India in the economic policy-making process using a bottom-up approach.
Its initiatives include:

  • 15-year road map
  • 7-year vision, strategy, and action plan
  • AMRUT
  • Digital India
  • Atal Innovation Mission
  • Medical Education Reform
  • Agriculture reforms (Model Land Leasing Law, Reforms of the Agricultural Produce Marketing Committee Act, Agricultural Marketing and Farmer Friendly Reforms Index for ranking states)
  • Indices Measuring States’ Performance in Health
  • Education and Water Management
  • Sub-Group of Chief Ministers on Rationalization of Centrally Sponsored Schemes
  • Sub-Group of Chief Ministers on Swachh Bharat Abhiyan
  • Sub-Group of Chief Ministers on Skill Development
  • Task Forces on Agriculture and up of Poverty

Listed below are some other initiatives taken by India:

Challenges

Despite the progress made, India still faces significant challenges in achieving the SDGs. India’s SDG preparedness ranking continues to decline, and its rank in the global Sustainable Development Report, 2022 released recently, slipped for the third consecutive year. Country’s preparedness in achieving the UN mandated SDGs by 2030 has worsened over the years in comparison with other countries.The UN report finds that India faces major challenges in 11 and significant challenges in 3 out of the 17 SDG goals. Challenges remains in one of the goals while only in two goal SDGs have somewhat been achieved. The country scored only 60.3 which is even below the regional average 65.9 for East and South Asia. The country ranked 121 out of 163 countries, a fall of one rank from 120th of 2021, and 4 ranks from 117th in 2020. Statistical performance index of India is only 70.4, and missing data in SDG index is about 1 per cent. The report was published by a group of independent experts at the Sustainable Development Solution Network (SDSN). The challanges faced are given and explained below

  • Poverty and inequality:Despite the country's rapid economic growth, poverty and income inequality remain significant challenges. Nearly one in five Indians still lives below the poverty line, which poses a significant obstacle to achieving several SDGs, including SDG 1 (No Poverty) and SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities).

  • Education:Despite progress in recent years, India still faces significant challenges in providing quality education to all. Many children, especially those from marginalized communities, still lack access to education and drop out early. This makes it difficult to achieve SDG 4 (Quality Education).

  • Healthcare:While India has made significant strides in improving healthcare, access to quality healthcare remains a challenge, particularly in rural areas. This poses an obstacle to achieving SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being).

  • Infrastructure:India's infrastructure, including transportation, water supply, and sanitation, is still inadequate in many parts of the country. This hampers progress towards several SDGs, including SDG 6 (Clean Water and Sanitation) and SDG 9 (Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure).

  • Gender inequality:Despite progress in recent years, gender inequality remains a significant challenge in India. Women are still underrepresented in several areas of society, including politics, education, and the workforce. This poses an obstacle to achieving SDG 5 (Gender Equality).

  • Environmental degradation:India faces significant challenges related to environmental degradation, including air pollution, deforestation, and climate change. These pose obstacles to achieving several SDGs, including SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy) and SDG 13 (Climate Action).

  • Urbanization:India's rapid urbanization has led to several challenges, including overcrowding, inadequate housing, and slums. This poses obstacles to achieving several SDGs, including SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities).


Consequences

Failing to attain the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by 2030 could result in dire consequences for India. These SDGs aim to eradicate poverty, promote sustainable development, and decrease inequalities. Failure to achieve these goals may result in increased poverty and inequality, environmental degradation, worsened health problems, lost economic opportunities, and social unrest. To achieve these goals, considerable investments in infrastructure, education, and healthcare are imperative. These investments will boost economic growth and create employment opportunities. Thus, it is vital for the government and other stakeholders to work together to achieve the SDGs and avoid any potential negative outcomes.


  • Increased poverty:India has the largest number of people living in extreme poverty, and not achieving the SDG goals would lead to an increase in poverty and inequality.

  • Environmental degradation:India is facing serious environmental challenges such as air pollution, water scarcity, and deforestation. Failure to achieve SDG goals would exacerbate these problems, leading to further environmental degradation and its consequences.

  • Health problems:India has a high burden of diseases such as malnutrition, tuberculosis, and non-communicable diseases. Not achieving the SDG goals would worsen the health problems in the country.

  • Economic consequences:Achieving the SDG goals would require significant investment in infrastructure, education, and healthcare, which would create jobs and stimulate economic growth. Failure to achieve these goals would result in lost economic opportunities.

  • Social unrest:Failure to achieve the SDG goals would lead to social unrest, as people become more frustrated with the government's inability to address their needs and aspirations.

In conclusion, failing to achieve the SDG goals would have serious consequences for India, both in terms of human development and economic growth. It is crucial for the government and other stakeholders to take concerted efforts to achieve these goals by 2030.

Hinduja Brothers

The Hinduja brothers, successful entrepreneurs and philanthropists, have made significant contributions towards achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in India. One of the main ways they have helped is through education. The Hinduja Foundation, established by the brothers, has set up schools, colleges, and vocational training centers to promote education and skill development. These efforts have contributed towards achieving SDG 4 - Quality Education.

Another area where the Hinduja brothers have made a positive impact is healthcare. The Hinduja Group has set up several hospitals and clinics across India to provide affordable healthcare services to the underprivileged. They have also supported medical research and development, including funding research into COVID-19. Through these initiatives, the Hinduja brothers have contributed towards achieving SDG 3 - Good Health and Well-being.



Renewable energy is another important area where the Hinduja brothers have invested heavily. They have set up several renewable energy projects across India, including wind and solar power, which have helped reduce carbon emissions and promote sustainable development. Through these initiatives, the Hinduja brothers have contributed towards achieving SDG 7 - Affordable and Clean Energy.

The Hinduja Foundation has also launched several initiatives to empower women. These include skill development programs and entrepreneurship training, as well as the creation of women's self-help groups to help women become financially independent. Through these efforts, the Hinduja brothers have contributed towards achieving SDG 5 - Gender Equality.



The attack of the storm Fani
In 2019, when Cyclone Fani struck the Indian state of Odisha, it left a trail of destruction in its wake. Many people lost their homes, and the region was left without access to basic necessities like food and water. The Hinduja Brothers, through their foundation, Hinduja Foundation, stepped in to provide relief to the affected communities.

The Hinduja Foundation team quickly mobilized and reached the affected areas to provide immediate relief. They set up relief camps and distributed food, water, and other essential supplies to the affected communities. The foundation also provided medical assistance to those who were injured during the cyclone.

One of the people who received help from the Hinduja Foundation was a man named Rakesh. Rakesh's house was completely destroyed during the cyclone, and he had lost all his possessions. He was left with nothing but the clothes on his back. When the Hinduja Foundation team arrived in his village, they provided him with food, water, and a temporary shelter to stay in.

Rakesh was overwhelmed by the kindness of the foundation and the Hinduja Brothers. He said, "I had lost all hope, but thanks to the Hinduja Foundation, I now have food to eat and a place to stay. I am grateful for their help."

The Hinduja Foundation continued to provide relief to the affected communities for several months after the cyclone. They helped rebuild homes, schools, and other infrastructure that had been damaged during the disaster. The foundation's efforts made a significant impact on the lives of many people who were affected by the cyclone.

Ramakrishna Mission Seva Pratishthan

Ramakrishna Mission Seva Pratishthan is a charitable organization that has been working towards the betterment of society in India since 1958. The organization has made significant contributions towards achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) in India through its various initiatives.

One of the primary ways the organization has helped is by providing healthcare services. The Seva Pratishthan operates a hospital that provides quality medical care to people from all sections of society, including the underprivileged. The hospital offers specialized medical care, including treatment for cancer, cardiac ailments, and other diseases. By providing affordable and accessible healthcare services, the organization has contributed towards achieving SDG 3 - Good Health and Well-being.



In addition to healthcare, Ramakrishna Mission Seva Pratishthan has also been actively involved in promoting education. The organization runs several educational institutions, including schools and colleges, where it provides quality education to students from different socio-economic backgrounds. By promoting education, the organization has contributed towards achieving SDG 4 - Quality Education.

Apart from healthcare and education, the organization has also been involved in several other initiatives that promote sustainable development. For example, the Seva Pratishthan has been involved in the distribution of solar lamps to remote and underprivileged areas, helping to achieve SDG 7 - Affordable and Clean Energy.

The organization has also launched several initiatives to empower women, including vocational training programs and self-help groups. By promoting gender equality and women's empowerment, the Seva Pratishthan has contributed towards achieving SDG 5 - Gender Equality.



The story of Preeti

One day, a young girl named Preeti was brought to the hospital run by Ramakrishna Mission Seva Pratishthan. Preeti had been suffering from a serious heart condition since birth and her family had been struggling to get her the treatment she needed. The family had visited several hospitals, but the cost of treatment was too high for them to afford.

When Preeti was brought to the Seva Pratishthan hospital, the doctors there diagnosed her condition and recommended that she undergo surgery as soon as possible. The family was worried about the cost of the surgery, but the hospital assured them that they would provide the treatment at a highly subsidized rate.

Thanks to the support of Ramakrishna Mission Seva Pratishthan, Preeti was able to undergo the surgery she needed. The operation was successful, and Preeti's health improved significantly. Her family was overjoyed and grateful to the hospital for the treatment they provided.

Preeti's story is just one example of the many lives that have been positively impacted by the services provided by Ramakrishna Mission Seva Pratishthan. The organization's commitment to providing quality healthcare services at an affordable cost has helped many people, especially those from underprivileged backgrounds, to access the medical care they need to live healthy and fulfilling lives.

Our Contribution

Being responsible citizens of this country, and most importantly the next generation of India, it is our duty to try and complete the SDGs. If we do not manage to complete the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), our generations will face an uncertain and challenging future. The SDGs are a global framework for addressing some of the world's most pressing issues, including poverty, hunger, inequality, climate change, and environmental degradation. If we fail to achieve these goals, we risk leaving a world that is more unequal, more polluted, and less resilient to shocks like pandemics and natural disasters.
The consequences of not achieving the SDGs will be particularly severe for the most vulnerable and marginalized communities, including children, women, and those living in poverty. These communities are already disproportionately affected by the negative impacts of global challenges like climate change and economic inequality, and failing to achieve the SDGs will only exacerbate these issues.
Without achieving the SDGs, we risk leaving future generations with a world that is less sustainable, less equitable, and less resilient to the challenges of the 21st century. It is therefore essential that we work together to achieve these goals and create a better future for ourselves. To contribute to the cause you can do things like,



  • Reduce, Reuse, Recycle: You can contribute to SDG 12 - Responsible Consumption and Production - by reducing your consumption of resources, reusing items, and recycling waste. You can also encourage others in your community to adopt sustainable practices.


  • Volunteer: You can volunteer your time and skills to organizations working towards achieving the SDGs in your community. This could include volunteering at a local NGO or participating in a community service project.


  • Speak up: If you see something in your community that is not sustainable or violates the SDGs, speak up and voice your concerns. You can write letters to your local government officials, participate in protests or campaigns, or use social media to raise awareness about the issue.


  • Plant trees: You can help to achieve SDG 15 - Life on Land - by planting trees and promoting reforestation efforts in your community. Trees are crucial for maintaining healthy ecosystems, combating climate change, and supporting biodiversity.


  • Save water: India is facing a water crisis, with many parts of the country experiencing water shortages. You can help to achieve SDG 6 - Clean Water and Sanitation - by conserving water in your daily life and encouraging others to do the same. Simple actions like turning off the tap while brushing your teeth or fixing leaky faucets can make a big difference.
